More ways to speed up your modem which do not require you to use Windows nor require any changes in your settings (mostly)

Prefetching
Yes, prefetching! It means that your browser downloads the next pages you're about to surf to while you're reading!
Okay, let me give you an example. You're in a page with 3 links from it. While you're reading this page, a special program downloads the next pages so when you'll finish reading and click on them they will be immedietly displayed.
I do not know which is the best prefetching program, but you can try your luck at Web Attack (find the surfing acceleration category).

Multi-Sockets Downloads
Big words, little mind? No problem, I'll explain. Basically a program that supports Multi-Sockets Downloads is downloading the files you tell it to in a rather... different way. Instead of opening one socket (the simplest explanation I can come up with is an "outgoing connection") and using it to download the file, Multi-Socket Downloaders use several sockets to connect to the file in different fragments ("pieces") of it and downloads them all at the same time. It even makes downloads from slow servers faster, since it deploys several connections. More information at http://www.lidan.com (this guy sucks at web design but his program rocks). The program is currently under development stages and I don't recommend using it since it's not very comfortable to use and it may even screw up your browser (it screwed up mine... ;-) ).
